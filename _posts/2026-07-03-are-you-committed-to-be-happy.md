> Let everything happen to you. Beauty and terror. Just keep going. No feeling is final.
>
> *Rainer Maria Rilke*

Are you committed to being happy, no matter what happens to you.

Not when things go your way. Not when the conditions line up and the day cooperates and everyone behaves the way you needed them to. Anyone can feel peaceful then. That is not a commitment, that is just good weather.

The real question is what happens when everything goes against you. When someone lets you down in the exact way you were afraid they would. When you lose something, or someone, you love. When the van breaks down in the middle of a roundabout and there is nowhere to pull over and everyone is watching. When the algorithm decides today is not your day, and you should stay quiet in your little corner of the internet, unseen.

Unless you make the decision ahead of time, your mind will manufacture suffering every single time life fails to match what you expected of it. It is not malicious. It is just what an unwatched mind does. It compares what is happening to what should be happening, finds the gap, and calls the gap suffering.

And that gap is a waste of the one life we actually have.

I am not talking about pretending the roundabout does not sting. It stings. I am talking about something underneath the sting, a floor that does not give way just because the day did. Rilke did not say let only the beautiful things happen to you. He said let everything happen. Beauty and terror both. Keep going anyway. No feeling, not even this one, is the last word.

That is the commitment. Not immunity. Not positive thinking. Just a refusal to hand your peace over.
