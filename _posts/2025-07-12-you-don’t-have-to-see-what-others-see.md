# You Don’t Have to See What Other People See

*On becoming an evolutionary rebel, not by fighting the world, but by finally looking at it through your own eyes.*

In the past six years I’ve slowly become my own kind of evolutionary rebel, simply and unapologetically by being me. Not a rebel for the sake of doing prohibited or illegal things, not a rebel with a badass attitude trying to screw powers that be. Something quieter and more radical than that.

Assuming my power. Saying no to what doesn’t resonate with my vital expression. Stepping out from under whoever or whatever wanted to dictate what is right or wrong, true or false, up or down for me to follow.

## The most revolutionary act

Looking at the world from your own eyes, and empowering others to do the same, is more of a revolutionary act than endlessly spending your energy against powers you don’t like and want off the face of the Earth.

We have no power over others’ freedom. Only over our own.

Everything is in its right place, as it needs to be. Energies don’t lie. Moral judgments are off.

## Believing in yourself unconditionally

We need to believe in ourselves and in our gifts, accepting who we are unconditionally and unapologetically, even if all the others around us look at us in despair and denial.

I invite you to look outside all your mechanical acts of dealing with reality and others, and leave them in a corner. Everything is possible from there. Are you ready to become the most authentic version of you? To let go of everything that isn’t aligned with your own soul evolution and watch it leave your life?

There is a simpler way than struggling.

## You don’t have to see what other people see

> “The first thing to grasp about what it is to be yourself is that you don’t have to see what other people see. That may sound simple but it’s really profound. You don’t have to see what other people see. Nobody can force you to see what you are not here to see. And yet most human beings are constantly conditioned to see things the way other people see them. But by design, we are all given a unique way by which to look at the world.”
> 
> *Ra Uru Hu*

That last line lands differently every time I read it. By design. Not by accident, not by effort, not by becoming someone else. By the very design of who you already are.

The rebel move isn’t fighting what’s out there. It’s coming back to the one set of eyes you were actually given.

I see you.
