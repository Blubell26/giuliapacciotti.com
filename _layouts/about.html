About

Giulia Pacciotti

Giulia Pacciotti is a seeker, inner work guide, and unconventional mother whose work sits at the intersection of psychological depth, spiritual sobriety, and the courage it takes to build a life that actually belongs to you.

In 2020, she packed up her life and left Italy to search for some clarity, with a deep knowing that the conventional version wasn't going to work for her. She set out on what Joseph Campbell would later help her name: the Hero's Journey. What followed was not a story with a clean arc. It was messier, truer, and brought her all the way to her knees and to the source.

Since then: unschooling her son, learning through life rather than a curriculum. Healing the body and the mind holistically without doctors. Going deep in the architecture of life. And writing honestly about what all of it actually looks like, without the spiritual ego or the polished Instagram version.

Her writing draws on depth psychology, shadow work, the inner child, and nervous system healing, rooted in years of study across traditions and thinkers — Gurdjieff, Sri Aurobindo, The Mother, Rilke, A.S. Neill, David Hawkins, Walsch, Leleu, and others. She writes to think. This path set her on a lifelong quest to uncover the mechanism behind the manipulation of human souls.

She is the founder of Keepers of the Wild Heart, a brand and community that holds all of this together: the parenting work, the inner work, the body healing, and the income sovereignty.

Giulia's work is for those willing to look at what they have been avoiding, question what they have been told, and take full responsibility for what comes next. Through long-form essays, online courses, and private work, she supports people who feel the pull toward something more honest, in how they parent, how they earn, how they heal, and how they know themselves.

In a time of increasing reality distortion, declining mental and physical health, endless distraction, and pervasive unconsciousness, Giulia's work helps people remember who they are, why they are here, and how to navigate what cannot be seen with courage, discernment, and an uncompromising commitment to truth.

This path isn't easier. But it's yours. Dorothy had the power the whole time. She just didn't know it, so she walked the whole road to Oz to be told what was already hers. I think about how much of what I've called an injustice in my own life was actually just me. Not someone taking my freedom. Me, never noticing it was sitting right there, waiting to be claimed.
— Giulia
